Choosing between Unifi and Maxis Home Fibre is one of the most common dilemmas facing Malaysian households today. Both are reputable providers, both offer fibre-optic connections, and both have competitive pricing. So how do you decide?

Here's an honest, side-by-side comparison to help you choose.

## Coverage: Where Each Provider Reaches

**Unifi** has the widest coverage in Malaysia by a significant margin. As TM's flagship product, Unifi leverages the country's largest telecommunications infrastructure with coverage reaching over **7.5 million premises** across Peninsular Malaysia, Sabah, and Sarawak. If you're in a suburban or rural area, Unifi is almost certainly your only fibre option.

**Maxis Home Fibre** has expanded its coverage substantially in recent years, but its reach is concentrated primarily in **major urban centres** — Klang Valley, Penang, Johor Bahru, and Kota Kinabalu. In many suburban and semi-rural areas, Maxis simply isn't available.

**Verdict:** If coverage is your concern, Unifi wins — especially outside major cities.

## Speed Plans and Pricing

Both providers offer competitive speeds, though the plan structures differ.

**Unifi Plans (2025):**
- 100Mbps — from RM89/month
- 300Mbps — from RM129/month
- 500Mbps — from RM149/month
- 1Gbps — from RM249/month
- 2Gbps — from RM319/month

**Maxis Plans (2025):**
- 100Mbps — typically from RM89/month
- 500Mbps — typically from RM149/month
- 1Gbps — typically from RM229/month

Pricing is broadly comparable at equivalent speed tiers. Unifi's 300Mbps plan at RM129/month is a standout value that Maxis doesn't directly match. Maxis sometimes offers slightly lower pricing at the 1Gbps tier.

**Verdict:** Similar pricing overall. Unifi's 300Mbps tier offers exceptional value.

## Contract and Commitment

Both providers typically require a **24-month contract**. Early termination fees apply if you cancel before the contract ends.

One area where Unifi has an edge: the **installation and hardware are included** in the monthly fee with no upfront costs. Maxis sometimes charges an activation fee or requires deposit depending on your credit profile.

## Equipment: Routers and Hardware

**Unifi** provides a Wi-Fi 6 Combo Box with most plans, and a Wi-Fi 7 Combo Box with Mesh for the 1Gbps and 2Gbps tiers. This means excellent coverage for most homes right out of the box.

**Maxis** also provides hardware with its plans, including Mesh Wi-Fi options with higher-tier plans.

**Verdict:** Both are comparable. Unifi's Wi-Fi 7 + Mesh on the 1Gbps plan is a strong offering.

## Customer Service and Support

This is where experiences vary most between users. Both providers receive mixed reviews for customer service — good when things work, frustrating when they don't.

Unifi benefits from TM's **extensive nationwide service network**, meaning technicians are available in more locations. Response times for fault reports have improved significantly in recent years, with most issues resolved within 24–48 hours.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Unifi if:**
- You're outside a major urban area (it may be your only option)
- You want the widest plan selection, especially the 300Mbps sweet spot
- You're bundling with Unifi TV or Unifi Mobile
- You want a proven, established provider with nationwide support

**Choose Maxis if:**
- You're in Klang Valley or a major city and Maxis is available at your address
- You already have Maxis Mobile and want a combined bill discount
- The specific Maxis 1Gbps pricing is significantly lower at your address

## The Bottom Line

For most Malaysian households — especially those outside major city centres — Unifi remains the default choice due to its unmatched coverage, strong plan lineup, and reliable fibre infrastructure. The 300Mbps plan at RM129/month represents outstanding value for a typical family of 3–5 people.
